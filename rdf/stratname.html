Description

An ontology for the Australian Stratnames dataset, with preliminary alignment to the Geological Timescale Ontology.

The ontology is based around the usual assumptions made in geological mapping - i.e. that the earth can be divided into 'units' that are distinguished from one another on lithological, chronological, geophysical, geochemical or other bases essentially connected with some aspect of their genesis.

Stratigraphic units are distinguished primarily on the basis of having been formed by some relatively uniform geologic process (and thus be composed of a relatively uniform lithological material) during a discrete time interval. Note that any particular occurence of a unit might not contain earth materials that cover the complete time span.

In the GA Stratnames dataset, the timing of the base and top (i.e. the max and min age) are specified as geologic eras - i.e. time-intervals - which implies a degree of uncertainty or variation in the exact temporal position of the boundary. Variation may be due to the terminating event applying at different times in different places.
